SECTION 1 — LEAVE POLICY
============================================================
Page 12

1.1 Annual Paid Leave
Employees are eligible for 24 paid leaves annually. Leave entitlement accrues at
2 days per month starting from the date of joining. Unused leave may be carried
forward up to a maximum of 12 days into the next calendar year.

1.2 Sick Leave
Employees are entitled to 10 sick days per year. Sick leave does not carry forward.
A medical certificate is required for absences exceeding 3 consecutive days.

1.3 Maternity / Paternity Leave
- Maternity leave: 26 weeks fully paid for female employees.
- Paternity leave: 5 working days for male employees, to be taken within 30 days
  of the child's birth.

1.4 Unpaid Leave
Unpaid leave may be granted at management's discretion for a maximum of 30 days
per year.

SECTION 2 — WORK-FROM-HOME POLICY
============================================================
Page 15

2.1 Eligibility
All permanent employees who have completed their probation period (90 days) are
eligible for up to 3 days of work-from-home per week.

2.2 Approval
WFH requests must be submitted through the HRMS portal at least 24 hours in advance.

SECTION 3 — PERFORMANCE REVIEW POLICY
============================================================
Page 18

3.1 Review Cycle
Performance reviews are conducted twice a year: in June and December.

3.2 Rating Scale
Employees are rated on a 5-point scale:
  5 – Exceptional
  4 – Exceeds Expectations
  3 – Meets Expectations
  2 – Needs Improvement
  1 – Unsatisfactory

3.3 Salary Revision
Salary revisions are linked to performance ratings and are effective from April 1
each year.

SECTION 4 — CODE OF CONDUCT
============================================================
Page 22

4.1 General Conduct
All employees are expected to maintain professional behaviour, respect colleagues,
and adhere to company values of Integrity, Innovation, and Inclusion.

4.2 Disciplinary Action
Violations of the code of conduct may result in a verbal warning, written warning,
suspension, or termination, depending on the severity.
